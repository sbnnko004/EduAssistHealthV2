Information for parents

 

A child leaving home for university can mark a new phase for the whole family. It is a time of excitement, anxiety and change.

The health and wellbeing pages provide a range of support and guidance for students, covering common life challenges, as well as physical and mental health needs.

We also want to help you, as a parent, in knowing what to expect and how we can help if your child is struggling.

In crisis now

Call  10111  for emergency services - or   for security services on campus call  (021) 650 2222/3  or  use the  Safe-Up UCT app .

Other sources of university support:

  • College welfare teams
  • University Departments
  • Student Support Services
  • Student welfare
  • Student finance
  • Disability Services
  • University Accommodation Services

You may experience a sense of loss at not having them at home and wish to contact them regularly. It's good to keep in touch, but they need to know that you trust them to make their own decisions (and sometimes mistakes).

Let them know they have your support and that they can talk to you if things are not going well. This will encourage self-confidence and independence, and they will be better able to manage the adjustment.

How can I expect to feel?

It's normal to worry about how your child will adapt to independent living and university life.

How can we help?

We encourage you to talk to your child directly. You can also raise concerns with your child's College Welfare Team.

If you have an urgent or immediate concern about your child's wellbeing it is best to contact the Security team on (021) 650 2222/3.
